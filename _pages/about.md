I am a PhD student at the Max Planck Institute for Human Cognitive and Brain Sciences, where I focus on making AI tools more trustworthy. My journey into this field began with a master's degree in electrical engineering, followed by experiences in both academia and industry. I briefly worked in Charley Wu's HMC Lab in Tübingen with Philipp Schwartenbeck and Peter Dayan, and served as an AI Engineer at Appsfactory in Leipzig before joining Nico Scherf's lab for my current research.

My primary research explores how we can enhance the reliability of Deep Reinforcement Learning (RL) algorithms. Specifically, I investigate methods to ensure these algorithms adhere to safety constraints, avoid risky behavior, and remain robust in the face of downstream changes or adversarial conditions during training. You can find a comprehensive review of trustworthy RL [here](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2209.08025).

I approach decision-making as a generative process, a perspective that allows me to leverage tools from representation learning. This includes techniques such as deep latent variable models, disentanglement, and the study of latent space geometry, which I adapt to address the unique challenges of RL. Through this lens, I aim to bridge theoretical insights with practical solutions for safer and more reliable AI systems.
