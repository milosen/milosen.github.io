I am a PhD student at the Max Planck Institute for Human Cognitive and Brain Sciences, where I focus on making AI tools more trustworthy. My journey into this field began with a master's degree in electrical engineering, followed by experiences in both academia and industry. 
I briefly worked in Charley Wu's [HMC Lab](https://hmc-lab.com/) in Tübingen 
and served as an AI Engineer at [Appsfactory](https://www.appsfactory.de/en) in Leipzig before joining [Nico Scherf's lab](https://www.cbs.mpg.de/methoden-und-entwicklungsgruppen/neuronale-datenwissenschaft-und-statistisches-rechnen) for my current research.

My primary research explores how we can enhance the reliability and trustworthiness of Deep Reinforcement Learning (RL) algorithms. Specifically, I investigate methods to ensure these algorithms adhere to safety constraints, avoid risky behavior, and remain robust in the face of downstream changes or adversarial conditions. You can find a comprehensive review of trustworthy RL [here](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2209.08025).
